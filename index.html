About Dragon Ball

Dragon Ball is a manga series by Japanese manga artist Akira Toriyama that was serialized in Weekly Shonen Jump magazine from 1984 to 1995.

The work is a long comic that tells the story of the adventure, effort, passion, and friendship centered around the protagonist Son Goku, who collects all seven beads scattered around the world to realize any wish - the secret treasure Dragon Ball.

The work has been made into a TV animation, broadcast on Fuji TV in Japan, and has also spawned theatrical animations, Hollywood movies, and many peripheral products and game software.

In 1995, the circulation of Weekly Shonen Jump, which serialized the manga, reached a record 6.53 million, and the circulation of the magazine decreased rapidly after the serialization of Dragon Ball ended. The cumulative circulation of the work, including the Dragon Ball Deluxe Edition, exceeded 160 million in Japan (as of 2016), with the Deluxe Edition reaching 20 million, and the global cumulative circulation exceeded 250 million (as of 2018). It is the most adapted into games in the world and has been included in the Guinness World Records. In 2009, it was selected as one of the "600 most influential books in China over the 60 years of New China" by the China Bookstore News and the China Publishing Science Research Institute.
